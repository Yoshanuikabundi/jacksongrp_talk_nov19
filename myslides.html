Protein structure forms according to a probability distribution

Ready for some maths?

The Boltzmann Distribution

\[ P_i = \frac{\exp(-\beta\epsilon_i)}{Z} \]

\(P_i\) is the probability of state \(i\)

\(\exp(x)\) is the exponential function \(e^x\)

\(\epsilon_i\) is the energy of state \(i\)

Which energy \(\epsilon_i\) represents depends on how state \(i\) is represented - Free energy for coarse grained representations - Hamiltonian for canonical ensemble in phase space - Potential energy for canonical ensemble in configuration space

\(\beta = \frac{1}{K_BT}\) incorporates the temperature

\[ \label{eqn:partition-function} Z = \sum_{i}\exp(-\beta\epsilon_i) \]
